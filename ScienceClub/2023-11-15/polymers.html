Core Concepts

  • Carbon is special because it commonly created bonds with itself and has 4 bonds, allows a very large number of molecules to be made with it.
  • Small molecules can be used as building blocks to make large molecules.
  • Macromolecules can contain thousands of atoms.
  • Most of the largest molecules are polymers.
  • The four major polymers in the human body are carbohydrates, proteins, DNA and fats.
  • The three major macronutrients in our diet are carbohydrates, proteins and fats.
  • Our bodies break down polymers into monomers which our bodies then use to make new polymers.

Terms

Forming a Peptide Bond


    As two amino acids come together, they form what is known as a "peptide bond".  In the process, a -OH group is removed from one amino acid and a hydrogen is removed from the other amino acid. Together the H- and the -OH form water.  Because this water is lost in the process of forming the peptide bond, this reaction is called a "dehydration reaction".

Hydrolysis


    When a peptide bond is broken, the hydrogen and the -OH groups that were lost during the formation of the peptide bond are put back onto the amino acids.  The hydrogen and -OH groups come from a water molecule which is broken apart during the reaction.  Breaking of water is called "hydrolysis", so this reaction is called a "hydrolysis reaction".

The Amino Acids


    There are 20 amino acids that make up the proteins in our bodies.  Each amino acid has a similar structure containing an amine group (-NH2), a carboxyl group (-COOH), and a side chain.  The side chains can be very different from each other.
