Core Concepts

  • Atoms form bonds with each other to combine to form molecules.
  • There are two main types of atomic bonds: covalent bonds and ionic bonds.
  • An ion forms when an atom gains or loses an electron.
  • Different atoms form different numbers of bonds.
  • Valence electrons are the electrons in the outer shell of atom.
  • The number of bonds an atom makes is dependent on its valence electrons.
  • Elements are grouped in columns in the periodic table by the number of valence electrons.
  • A noble gas has a full outer electron shell and does not form bonds with other elements.
  • The elements in the first column form 1 bond.
  • Atoms can form single
  • There are several different ways to draw the structure of a molecule.

A molecule is made of two or more atoms that are chemically bonded together.



A compound is a substance made of all the same molecule.



The electrons in an atom's outer shell. The valence electrons are the ones that are chemically active and form bonds between atoms.



An atom that has gained or lost electrons. If an atom loses an electron it becomes a positively-charged ion. If an atom gains an electron it becomes a negatively-charged ion.



The bond between two ions. An ionic bond is not as strong as a covalent bond.



A covalent bond forms when two atoms share electrons. It is stronger than an ionic bond.



A covalent molecule that has covalent bonds between its atoms. A covalent molecule will not break apart when the molecule dissolves in water.
