Core Concepts

  • There are three main types of blood cells: red blood cells, white blood cells and platelets.
  • Red blood cells carry oxygen from the lungs to the rest of the body.
  • White blood cells fight off infections.
  • Platelets help make blood clots to stop bleeding.
  • Looking at a person's blood under the microscope can help determine what disease they have.
  • Red blood cells are red due to the hemoglobin that they contain.
  • Hemoglobin holds on to the oxygen that the red blood cells carry.
