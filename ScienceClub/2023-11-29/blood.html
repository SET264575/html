Core Concepts

  • There are three main types of blood cells: red blood cells, white blood cells and platelets.
  • Red blood cells carry oxygen from the lungs to the rest of the body.
  • White blood cells fight off infections.
  • Platelets help make blood clots to stop bleeding.
  • Looking at a person's blood under the microscope can help determine what disease they have.
  • Red blood cells are red due to the hemoglobin that they contain.
  • Hemoglobin holds on to the oxygen that the red blood cells carry.

Pictures

Normal Blood


    This is a normal peripheral blood smear.

Anisocytosis


    This is a blood smear that shows red blood cells of different sizes, a condition known as anisocytosis.  In this case, it is caused development of new, normally-sized red blood cells in a patient who had anemia with small red blood cells.

Iron Deficiency Anemia


    This blood smear shows red blood cells that are pale due to a lack of hemoglobin.  The patient did not have enough iron and therefore could not make the hemoglobin that was needed to fill the red blood cells.

A Bird's Red Blood Cells


    Unlike in mammals, the red blood cells in birds keep their nuclei.

Poikilocytes


    Diseases of red blood cells often cause them to have different shapes which can be seen under a microscope.

Schistocytes


    Schistocytes are red blood cells that have a damaged and torn cell membrane.

Sickle Cells


    Sickle cells are caused by abnormal hemoglobin that sticks together in long chains that push the cell membrane into long, curved shapes.
