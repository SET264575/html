Solar Eclipse

Terms to Know

See if you know these terms, then click on the buttons to reveal the definitions.


A solar eclipse occurs when the moon covers up part of the sun. So, the moon is between the earth and the sun.



A lunar eclipse occurs when the moon passes through the shadow of the earth. So, the earth is between the moon and the sun.



The path that a moon takes around a planet, or the path that a planet takes around the sun.



An oval. The shape of the orbit of a moon or planet.



A transit occurs when an object passes in front of another object. Often, this refers to a satellite or a planet such as Mercury or Venus passing in front of the sun. When the sun is completely (or almost completely) covered up, the transit is called an eclipse.



The point in the orbit at which the moon (or a satellite) is farthest from the earth.



The point in the orbit at which the moon (or a satellite) is nearest to the earth.



When the moon is near its apogee, it appears smaller in the sky than usual, and smaller than the sun. If it passes over the sun during that time, it will not completely cover the sun and a ring of the sun's light can be seen around the moon.



A partial eclipse occurs when the moon passes in front of the sun but the center of the moon never lines up with the center of the sun.



A total eclipse occurs when the moon completely covers the sun.

A time sequence of pictures of an annular solar eclipse

      This picture is actually a series of images that have been merged together.  The photographer took multiple pictures through the eclipse.  Because the 
      Earth turns during that time, the sun appears to move across the sky.  The beginning of the eclipse is in the upper left.  As the moon moves across the
      sun, the sun is setting at the same time.  You can see the sun touching the horizon in the bottom right corner.

Transit of Venus

      A transit is like a mini-eclipse.  This is a sequence of pictures taken of the planet Venus crossing in front of the sun.  If Venus were much bigger or
      much closer, it would cover more of the sun and we would consider it an eclipse.  Because so very little of the sun is covered by Venus, we call it a transit,
      but it's basically the same thing.  A transit of Venus is very rare.

Transit of the International Space Station

      This is the International Space Station going across the sun.  It is important to remember that the ISS is very small but also very close to us.  
      From the sun's point of view, the ISS would not be noticable at all and it would not seem that the ISS was "crossing the sun".  

      If you look closely, you can see the shape of some of the structures on the ISS.
