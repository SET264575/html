General Teaching Points To Share In Results Email

General Teaching Points

Documenting interpretation of EKGs and imaging

It was noted by several reviewers that if you document your own interpretation of an EKG
or xray, you should make it clear that it is your interpretation, if it is.
You should use some unambiguous way to note that it is your work, such as "per my interpretation" or
"my interpretation of the xray is".

Documenting teleradiology reads

You should also copy the interpretation of teleradiology reads and put them in your note, and
indicate the interpretation is from teleradiology. Otherwise this read will not be easy to find
and the Boone Hospital radiologist's interpretation, which may differ from the interpretation you were
working with at the time, may be the only interpretation that can be found (at least without great difficulty).

Acknowledging abnormal troponins

Now that we are using high-sensitivity troponins, we are seeing a lot more that are slightly elevated.
Several reviewers noted the importance of commenting on the suspected cause of the abnormal troponin
rather than not acknowledging it. Often this is as simple as stating that you suspect the troponin is
elevated due to CKD or CHF.
