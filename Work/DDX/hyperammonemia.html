• Hyperammonemia
    • Acquired
      • Hepatic
        • Type A hepatic encephalopathy - acute liver failure (includes synthetic dysfunction)
          •      viral hepatitis
          •      ischemic hepatitis
          •      hepatotoxin ingestion
        • Type B hepatic encephalopathy - portosystemic shunts with no underlying liver disease
          •      congenital (rarely)
          •      artificially created for portal vein thrombosis
        • Type C hepatic encephalopathy - cirrhosis and portal hypertension due to:
          •      chronic hepatitis B or C
          •      biliary atresia
          •      alpha 1 antitrypsin deficiency
          •      Wilson disease
          •      cystic fibrosis
        • Acutely precipitated by:
          •      renal failure
          •      GI bleeding
          •      infection
      • Nonhepatic
        • Hematologic disorders
          •      multiple myeloma
          •      acute leukemia
        • Infections
          • urease-producing organisms
            •      Proteus mirabilis
            •      E coli
            •      Klebsiella
        • Unmasked urea cycle defects (heterozygous females & males with residual function, fairly common)
          • increased muscle catabolism
            •      seizure
            •      starvation
          • increased protein load
            •      TPN
            •      GI bleeding
            •      infection
        • Drugs
          •      valproic acid
          •      topiramate
          •      carbamazepine
          •      salicylates
          •      sulfadiazine
    • Congenital
      • Enzyme defects
        •      Urea cycle defects
        •      Organic acidemias
        •      Congenital lactic acidosis
        •      Fatty acid oxidation defect
        •      Dibasic amino acid deficiencies
      •      Transient hyperammonemia of the newborn
      •      Neonatal HSV infection
      •      Severe perinatal asphyxia
