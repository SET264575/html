• Anasarca
    • An elevation in capillary hydraulic pressure
      • Heart failure
        •      Cardiac tamponade resulting in heart failure and generalized edema
        •      Constrictive pericarditis with associated heart failure
        •      Cor pulmonale resulting in right heart failure
        •      Dilated cardiomyopathy
      • Kidney disease
        •      Acute renal failure, especially oliguric renal failure with fluid retention
        •      Chronic kidney disease (advanced stages with fluid retention)
    •      Early cirrhosis
    •      Pregnancy
    •      Eclampsia (severe eclampsia)
    •      Drugs
    • Venous obstruction or insufficiency states
      •      DVT
      •      hepatic venous congestion
  • Increased capillary permeability
    •      Burns
    •      Trauma
    •      Sepsis
    •      Allergic reactions
    •      Angioedema, rarely, when it is severe and generalized
    •      Capillary leak syndrome
    •      Malignant ascites
  • Lymphatic obstruction
    •      Malignancy
    •      Post lymph node dissection
  • Hypoalbuminemia
    •      Nephrotic syndrome
    •      Liver disease
    •      Malnutrition
  • Medications
    •      Calcium channel blockers usually cause localized edema and not anasarca
    •      Diazoxide usually cause localized edema and not anasarca
    •      Corticosteriods
