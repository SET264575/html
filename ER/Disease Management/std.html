Syphilis screening

The order for a "Syphilis screen" does a screening test and the reflexes to confirmatory tests. The screening test is resulted as "Treponema pallidum Ab" which is confusing becauses this seems to be a Treponema-specific test that would not require confirmation. However, additional tests are needed to confirm that this truly represents active syphilis infection. These confirmatory tests, which take about 5 days to result, include "T.pallidum Ab (TP-PA), as well as "RPR" and "RPR Titer/FTA". A negative RPR suggests that the antibody test may represent a previously-treated case of syphilis. The Boone Hospital lab states that they will report the patient to the health department only after the confirmatory tests are available and positive.
